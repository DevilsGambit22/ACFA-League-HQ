How the League Works

1. Review: Check official league announcements, schedules, and event instructions.
2. Coordinate: Club representatives communicate with League Administration and their teams.
3. Compete: Approved clubs participate in official Chess.com league events.
4. Record: Results, standings, awards, and season history are maintained by League HQ.

League Rules & Operations

ACFA League HQ members are approved representatives and league personnel. Use this section as the quick-reference center for official expectations during the season.

Sportsmanship: Treat opponents, organizers, and fellow representatives with respect.
Representation: Each club must maintain an authorized representative who can receive and relay official information.
Event Compliance: Clubs must follow published schedules, formats, eligibility requirements, and event instructions.
Disputes: Concerns should be submitted privately to League Administration with clear supporting information.
Official Decisions: Commissioner and League HQ rulings govern league operations and recorded results.

The Future of ACFA League

ACFA League HQ is being restructured as an administrative organization rather than a second general community club. ACFA Official remains the public community hub, while League HQ manages registered clubs, representatives, schedules, standings, rules, appeals, awards, and championship archives.

Community: ACFA Official remains open to players and supporters.
Administration: League HQ remains focused on approved representatives and league personnel.
Competition: Official events take place on Chess.com under a consistent league format.
Legacy: Results, awards, champions, and club histories are preserved.
